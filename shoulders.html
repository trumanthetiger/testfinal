SHOULDERS

Structure

Shoulders are a part of the body that is uniquely suited to shrugging. They are located between the head and the knees, and are often used to carry heavy bags, wear backpacks, and generally support the weight of the world on your shoulders. Cool, huh?

Function

The primary function of shoulders is to allow the arms to move in a wide range of directions. Without shoulders, we would not be able to perform many everyday tasks, such as reaching for objects, hugging loved ones, or scratching that itch on our backs.

Development

Similar to heads, shoulder's are created when you are born. So Cool!

Interesting Facts

Here are some fun facts about shoulders that you might not know:
  • My shoulders hurt right now..
  • The shoulder joint is one of the most mobile joints in the human body, allowing for a wide range of movements, including shrugging.
  • Shoulders are the only part of the body that can rotate a full 360 degrees.
So next time you shrug your shoulders, remember just how amazing they really are!
